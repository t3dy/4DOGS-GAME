# Scene Brainstorm — sketches not yet built

**Status:** SPECULATIVE. A running list of scene ideas extrapolated from real dog behavior, where we have NOT yet observed the specific behavior in Ted's pack. Don't promote any of these to canon without checking with Ted first. They're notes, not commitments.

Voice: Ted's plain conversational voice if/when any of these get built (see `feedback_dogsgame_voice.md`).

## Routines & ritual

### Vet visit
The car ride to the vet. Different dogs read the cues differently. Crockett goes most often (senior care). Outcome: how much do you medicate / leash-prep / treat-bait?

### Bath time
Aussies particularly hate it. Where does each dog hide? Crockett may need lifting. Attila is just a wet wall.

### Nail clipping
Refusal mechanic. Some dogs need two people. Some dogs need a peanut-butter distractor. Score: nails clipped before the dog had enough.

### Brushing / grooming
Aussie undercoat is a chore. Some dogs love it, some endure it. Could be a slow-tick mini-encounter with progress meter.

### Coming home for the second time today
Different from the first homecoming (B-greeting_ritual [spec]). Lower energy. The dogs grade you on whether you brought groceries.

### Bedtime ritual
Each dog has their station. Who gets walked to bed? Who self-deploys? Who tries to negotiate?

## Food & resource

### Treat distribution fairness
Three dogs are sitting attentively. The fourth is asleep in another room. Do you save him a treat? What happens when he finds out?

### Mealtime
Four bowls, four feeding stations, one small kitchen. The race / line / negotiation. Crockett is fed last (slow chewer). Or first (because he's old)?

### Leftovers from a plate
Begging dynamics differ per dog. Attila stares from a polite distance. Okie circles. Tex stares. Crockett is asleep but he can hear it.

### Picky eating
A new food. Two dogs eat it. Two don't. Now what?

### Drop something on the kitchen floor
Five-second rule contest. Whoever's closest wins.

## Threshold events

### Doorbell at night
Different stakes than B-stranger_at_door [spec] daytime version. You're in pajamas. You don't want to open it. The dogs disagree.

### Garage door rises while a dog is too close
Mild peril. Okie's been known to stand right under it. Recovery options: pull him back, abort, scold yourself.

### Open the back door for one dog and the wrong dog comes through
Triage at the threshold. A small-stakes Choose-Your-Outcome.

### Carrying groceries through three dogs
Physical comedy as logistics. Where do you put them down? Who tries to investigate the bag?

## Inter-dog dynamics (extending B017-B019)

### Tex defends Okie from a strange dog at the park
Or doesn't. Either is canonical for older brothers.

### Crockett sleeps in Okie's spot in the garage
Okie arrives. The spot is occupied. Refuge negotiation.

### Attila demonstrates resource value
He carries something around to make the others want it. Counter-toy-economy.

### The four-dog standoff
All four want the same warm spot on the rug. Who wins?

## Environmental triggers

### Distant fireworks
A summer evening. Heard but not seen. Anxiety differential — Okie spikes, Crockett mostly sleeps through, Attila looks confused, Tex is alert but okay.

### Power outage
Sudden quiet. The dogs notice. The fridge stops humming and Crockett sits up.

### Smoke alarm test
The chirp. The dogs react variably. Two minutes of disturbance.

### Storm rolling in
Barometric pressure drop. Anxiety climbs. Where do they go? (The answer is the garage. It's always the garage.)

### Snow on the ground
Sultan, WA gets snow. Different dogs have different relationships to snow.

## Health & age

### Crockett can't get up easily today
Arthritis is worse some days. The morning starts slow. Help or wait?

### Okie's anxiety on a bad day
Some days are bad. The mechanic differs from a normal anxiety day — the floor is higher.

### A small wound on Tex
Don't know how it happened. Vet or wait?

### Attila ate something he shouldn't have
The aftermath. Yard inspection. Worry.

## Travel / car

### Loading the dogs into the car for a trip
Choreography. Order matters. Who gets the back? Who gets shotgun?

### Long drive
Hour 3. Everyone is restless. A rest stop.

### Coming home from boarding
Reunion. Different than daily homecoming. They're suspicious of you for a few hours.

### A friend's house
Different smells, different rules. Who explores first? Who stays glued to you?

---

## Edge cases

### Visitor brings their own dog
Inter-pack dynamics. Hierarchy negotiation. Ted in the middle.

### Someone in the household is sick
The dogs know. They behave differently. Quieter, closer.

### Moving day
Boxes everywhere. The dogs are unsettled. Encounters get shorter.

### A new piece of furniture
Investigation. Marking risk. Sniff-and-decide loops.

## How to use this list

When inspiration strikes for the next encounter:
1. Pick one that fits the day's mood.
2. Check it against `behaviorresearch.md` to see if the science is captured.
3. Confirm the specific behavior is real for Ted's dogs (or is a reasonable extrapolation).
4. If it's unobserved, note that in the encounter (use the `[spec]` tag in `SCENE_INDEX`).
5. Build a tight encounter. 4 options. Plain voice.
6. Add to this file's "BUILT" section so it doesn't get rebuilt.
